Compound Interest is the process of calculating interest on an initial principal amount as well as the accumulated interest over time. Unlike simple interest, where interest is only calculated on the principal, compound interest takes into account interest on both the initial investment and the interest previously earned.

Compound Interest Calculator Instruction Manual

To use the Compound Interest Calculator, follow these steps:

  1. Principal Amount ($): Enter the initial amount of money you are investing or loaning (e.g., 1000).
  2. Annual Interest Rate (%): Enter the interest rate in percentage terms (e.g., 5 for 5%).
  3. Times Compounded Per Year: Specify how many times the interest is compounded per year (e.g., 12 for monthly compounding).
  4. Number of Years: Enter the number of years for which the money will grow (e.g., 10).
  5. Click Calculate to get the final amount after the specified period.

The tool will then calculate the total amount accumulated, including both the initial principal and the interest accrued over time, based on the formula:

Amount = P(1 + r/n)^(nt)

Where:

  • P: Principal amount
  • r: Annual interest rate (in decimal form)
  • n: Number of times the interest is compounded per year
  • t: Time in years

The final amount will be displayed on the screen after calculation.
